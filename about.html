About Rois Cordova

Photographer, visual storyteller, and explorer of light

My Story & Vision

My journey into photography began as a simple fascination with capturing moments, but quickly evolved into a lifelong passion for visual storytelling. As a photographer based in Phoenix, I've developed a distinctive style that balances technical precision with creative intuition, allowing me to capture the extraordinary within everyday scenes.

What drives my work is the belief that photography is more than documentation—it's a powerful medium for connection and emotion. Whether I'm photographing the vibrant urban landscapes of Thailand, the delicate details of a flower, or the breathtaking colors of a sunset, my vision remains consistent: to reveal the hidden beauty in ordinary moments and transform the fleeting into the permanent.

My artistic approach is guided by a deep appreciation for natural light, authentic emotion, and thoughtful composition. Every image I create aims to tell a story, evoke feeling, and invite viewers to see the world through a different lens—one where beauty exists in unexpected places, waiting to be discovered.

Achievements & Projects

Throughout my career, I've had the privilege of working on diverse projects that have challenged and expanded my creative vision. From capturing the bustling streets of Thailand to documenting the subtle interplay of light during golden hour in the Arizona desert, each project has contributed to my growth as an artist.

My work has been featured in several local exhibitions, where my series on natural patterns and organic forms received particular recognition. As a photographer who values both technical excellence and artistic expression, I continually push myself to explore new techniques, perspectives, and storytelling approaches.

Recent projects have included a photographic study of architectural geometry, an ongoing series documenting changing urban landscapes, and collaborative work with local artists. Each of these endeavors reflects my commitment to visual exploration and artistic growth, while maintaining the authentic perspective that defines my work.

Connect With Me

🌏 A Thoughtful Approach to Tech, Creativity, and Living I’ve been working in computer science since the mid-90s, moving through roles in software development, systems architecture, cloud infrastructure, and AI. Over the years, I’ve learned how to stay ahead of emerging technologies without losing sight of clarity and purpose. My work has always been about more than just code—it’s about creating systems that make sense, scale well, and leave things better than I found them. One of the contributions I’m proud of is a peer-to-peer software distribution system I helped develop and patent. It laid the groundwork for more efficient content delivery and has shown up in technologies that support decentralized and scalable infrastructure. But what motivates me now isn’t just optimization—it’s finding tools and approaches that fit into more intentional workflows. 👨‍💻 Keeping AI Grounded These days, I’m focused on using AI in ways that are actually useful—not just impressive on paper. I like platforms that encourage modularity and collaboration between tools without unnecessary complexity. For me, AI should serve the user, not the other way around. I tend to avoid overly abstract setups and gravitate toward systems that are simple, maintainable, and well-aligned with the work at hand. 🌅 Shifting Gears I’m in a phase of life where I’m designing things more on my own terms. That includes how I spend my time, where I live, and what I choose to work on. I still love solving hard problems, but I’ve shifted away from high-pressure timelines and rigid structures. I prefer slower mornings, creative sessions in outdoor cafés, and projects that leave space for reflection and iteration. This isn’t about checking out—it’s about showing up differently. I want my work to be intentional, rooted in curiosity, and aligned with how I want to live. 📸 Visual Exploration Photography has become one of my creative outlets. I shoot with a Sony mirrorless setup and usually keep the settings wide open to get a smooth, shallow depth of field. I like working with natural light and subtle edits that bring out the contrast and mood without overdoing it. When it comes to visual styles, I’m drawn to Futurism, Fauvism, and expressive realism. I like art that says something—work that feels alive, unexpected, and just a little bit strange in the right ways.
